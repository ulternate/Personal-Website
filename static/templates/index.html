SlackCAH
Play Cards Against Humanity with your Slack team

2016

To improve my Ruby skills for my Diploma studies, and because the Diploma team was using Slack to communicate, I built a SlackBot to play Cards Against Humanity, and then posted it on GitHub

I built the game-logic / commands whilst using slack-ruby-bot to handle the connection to our Slack board. The bot works on Heroku, allowing team members to play whenever they want.

Players can start a game with designated players, pick their card(s) to play each round and reprompt the bot if they've forgotten their cards or the question. The bot can also show the status of other players so you can nag them to play/pick, it can tell you the currenty points tally and quit/restart the game on your command. One player each round is the card czar responsible for picking the winning hand, they aren't allowed to play their own hand that round.

I've created a configuration guide to help others set it up on their own Slack Team and channel. The guide also includes installation information for running the bot on Heroku. For more info about the bot, see my repository on GitHub.

KFL.com.au
An Australian Rules Football Fantasy League homepage

2015 - 2016

The KFL is a local Australian Rules Football fantasy league which had been running a simple website since the 2014 AFL Season.

I was approached to improve the functionality of their website, support more screen sizes, allow user sign-up and add team selection for their upcoming 2016 competition. The user sign-up and team selection was desired to enable competitors to play without having to email their team selections through to an admin.

In previous seasons the site content was edited by manually editing html files as such the client's main request was to be able to easily update the site. I rebuilt the site using Django for dynamically serving content and enabling updates through an admin console. I also used Bootstrap as a base to support mobile and tablet screen sizes.

The site was finished ahead of schedule, giving the client ample time to prepare for the 2016 AFL season.

StevenCJones.com.au
A landing page for a local wedding/portrait photographer hosted on Google App Engine

2015

A local wedding/portrait photographer requested a simple landing page to develop his web presence and show his latest photoshoots.

He wanted the page to be clean, simple and needed the site to be live that weekend to coordinate the launch with his new Facebook business page.

From the initial discussions at a lunch meeting the site was built and hosted, via Google App Engine, by the end of the day 5 hours later. Stevencjones.com.au was live in time for the launch of the client's new Facebook business page.

The photo portfolio shows low resolution images on page load, however clicking on an image brings up a slideshow showing off the high resolution photo's to potential customers. Bootstrap was used to achieve a responsive layout across desktop, tablet and mobile devices.

Loyalist
A Loyalty card application for Android

2014 - 2016

I developed Loyalist starting in 2014 to de-clutter my wallet. I always forgot my loyalty cards and missed out on the points and rewards when getting a meal or going to the movies.

The app now allows for multiple digital wallets, supports most major barcode types and allows for manual card entry for cards without barcodes. You can also save notes to each card and search your cards across all your wallets.

The app is available on all Android phones version 4.0.3 and up. I also built a website for discovery and support of the app visit getloyalist.com.au for more info, or you can find it on the Google Play Store using the button below.

Inspection Suite
A collection of VBA programs for Engineering Inspections

2011 - 2016

At WSP | Parsons Brinckerhoff we completed various inspections for clients of their mining and production assets for compliance to standards and to identify defects requiring remediation.

Initially inspections were completed by engineers using pen & paper on-site, requiring costly clean-up work back in the office to produce reports. To reduce these costs I built a variety of applications in Excel using VBA that allow for the full site inspection to be captured digitally (including photos, condition ratings, remedial tasks, minutes of meeting, drawings).

The applications also generate pdf reports for the client at the conclusion of the inspection. Simplifying and automating the report generation further reduced costs associated with our inspections improving our likelihood of winning future work.
